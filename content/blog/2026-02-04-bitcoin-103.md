<b>Why should I care about Cold Storage?</b>

<p>People have lost hundreds of millions in Bitcoin. These are the most common reasons:</p>

<ul>
    <li><b>Lost seed phrase:</b> <a class="link-text" target="_blank" href="https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c74zel3ng1vo">James Howells</a> accidentally threw away a hard drive containing 8,000 Bitcoin (now worth over 700 million euros) in 2013.</li>
    <li><b>Scams or phishing attacks:</b> <a class="link-text" target="_blank" href="https://heraldsheets.com/protocol-podcast-host-loses-bitcoin-btc-savings/">Eric Savics</a> lost 7 years of savings (over 1 million euros in Bitcoin) in 20 minutes after downloading a fake wallet app.</li>
    <li><b>Exchange hacks/collapse:</b> The <a class="link-text" target="_blank" href="https://www.investopedia.com/what-went-wrong-with-ftx-6828447">FTX collapse</a> in 2022 left customers unable to access billions in Bitcoin they stored on the exchange.</li>
    <li><b>Death without an inheritance plan:</b> Family members unable to <a class="link-text" target="_blank" href="https://www.swanbitcoin.com/education/multi-sig-custody-and-inheritance/">access Bitcoin after the owner's death</a>, lost forever.</li>
</ul>

<p>The pattern: Most losses are from not having proper backups or giving up custody to third parties. This is why cold storage is essential, especially when you have a significant amount of Bitcoin.</p>

<b>What is Cold Storage?</b>

<p>Cold storage is the practice of securing Bitcoin offline, away from internet-connected devices and potential hackers. It's the standard for protecting significant amounts of Bitcoin you plan to hold long-term.</p>

<p>As we saw in <a class="link-text" target="_blank" href="https://cosmevalera.dev/blog/bitcoin-102/"><b>BTC 102: How to Buy Bitcoin Securely</b></a>, a hot wallet is a wallet that has had its seed phrase generated online, it's convenient, but also potentially vulnerable. A cold wallet, on the other hand, is a wallet that has had its seed phrase generated offline, and where the seed phrase has never been exposed to any online device, thus it is immune to online hacks. However, this security comes with responsibility: your Bitcoin is only safe as long as you don't share the private seed and don't lose it.</p> 

<b>When should I move to Cold Storage?</b>

<p>There is no hard rule for when to move to cold storage, but it's usually said that you should consider it when you have around 2 salaries invested in Bitcoin.</p>

<p>Hardware wallets cost around 100-250 euros, plus the cost of a backup system for your seed phrase (around 50-100 euros). If you only have 100 euros in Bitcoin, the investment may not be justified yet. However, some people prefer the peace of mind of going cold storage from the beginning, it's a matter of personal preference. A good rule of thumb: if you're losing sleep worrying about your Bitcoin security, it's time to move to cold storage.</p>

<h4>Hardware Wallet</h4>

<p>A hardware wallet is a physical device that generates and stores your wallet seed offline, creating what's known as a cold wallet. This way it grants maximum security to our Bitcoin. </p>

<p>The device allows you to sign transactions offline before broadcasting them to the network, ensuring your private keys never leave the secure environment of the hardware wallet, even when sending Bitcoin. Modern hardware wallets also offer additional security features, which won't be covered in this tutorial but are worth learning about on your own, such as: temporary seeds, passphrases, multi-signature support, etc.</p>

<p>There are different options for your hardware wallet (ColdCard, BitBox02, Trezor, Jade), each one of them has a different price and sometimes different specifications. I recommend using ColdCard, either the <a class="link-text" target="_blank" href="https://coldcard.com/mk4"><b>ColdCard Mk4</b></a> (around 150 euros) which is the device I'll use for this tutorial, or pay a little bit more to have a bigger screen and more buttons: <a class="link-text" target="_blank" href="https://coldcard.com/q"><b>ColdCard Q</b></a> (around 250 euros). Both options are great.</p>

This product consists of 2 metal plates and a punch pen to record your seed phrase (you can store up to two different seed phrases). </p>

<h4>Installing Sparrow Wallet</h4>

<p>Install <a class="link-text" target="_blank" href="https://sparrowwallet.com/download/"><b>Sparrow Wallet</b></a> on your computer. Sparrow is an open-source and non-custodial desktop wallet especially designed for cold storage of Bitcoin. It's what most Bitcoiners use to manage their hardware wallets.</p>

<p>The initial setup is straightforward:</p>

- Install <a class="link-text" target="_blank" href="https://sparrowwallet.com/download/"><b>Sparrow Wallet</b></a>
- [Introduction] **Click Next**
- [Public Server] **Click Next**
- [Bitcoin Core node] **Click Next**
- [Connecting to a Private Electrum Server] **Configure Server**
- **Click Test Connection**
- If it says "Connected to Electrum": **Click Create New Wallet**
  - If it failed, pick another Public Server URL and try again
- **Now Sparrow Wallet is set up!** 

<p>We'll import your cold wallet from the hardware wallet in the next steps. For now, Sparrow is ready and waiting.</p>

<h4>Backup your seed phrase in metal</h4>

<p>The backup card that came with your ColdCard is only meant for temporary storage during setup. Now we'll transfer your seed phrase to a permanent, fireproof, and waterproof metal backup.</p>

<p><b>Creating your metal backup:</b></p>

- Unpack the OneKey KeyTag (includes two metal plates, a punch pen, and instructions)
- Use the punch pen to carefully punch the holes corresponding to each word of your seed phrase
- Double-check your work (each word must be punched correctly)
- Optional: Add an NFC chip to store a note (like "Bitcoin wallet backup")
- Optional: Apply the tamper-evident seal (wait until after you've verified the backup works)

<p><b>‚ö†Ô∏è Critical security steps:</b></p>

- **Never photograph** your seed words or backup card
- **Don't let anyone see** your seed phrase while you're working
- Once you've punched the metal plate, **burn or shred** the paper backup card completely

<p><b>Verify your backup before destroying the paper:</b></p>

Before destroying the backup card, it's wise to test your metal backup. You can do this by temporarily deleting your wallet from Sparrow and restoring it using only the metal plate. This ensures that if you ever need to recover your wallet years from now, you'll be able to. Don't wait 10 years to discover your backup doesn't work!

<p><b>Store your metal plate securely:</b></p>

<p>Your metal plate is now the master key to your Bitcoin. Anyone who finds it can access your funds. Consider storing it in a home safe or other secure location. Remember: the ColdCard stores the wallet in its secure element, so for day-to-day use you won't need the metal plate, it's only for recovery if your ColdCard is lost or damaged.</p>
